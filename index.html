Sustainable webdesign 101

10 Basic rules for creating a sustainable website

#1 Colors
Choose colors with a low carbon footprint: Some colors require more energy to display than others. For example, black requires less energy than white, so using a black background can help reduce energy consumption. Additionally, using darker colors can also help reduce energy consumption because they require less light to be displayed.
So how much does each RGB color use in full brightness?
Red: 600mW.
Green: 580mW.
Blue: 800mW.
#2 Pictures
To shrink picture files for a sustainable website, you can:
Compress your images using online tools like TinyPNG or software programs like Adobe Photoshop. Choose the right image format, like JPEG for photographs or PNG for logos or illustrations. Reduce image dimensions before uploading them to your website. Optimize image quality to balance image quality with file size. Use responsive images to adjust image size and resolution based on the user's device and screen size.
#3 Videos
To shrink video files for a sustainable website, you can:
Compress your video files using tools like Handbrake or Adobe Media Encoder. Choose efficient video formats like MP4 or WebM instead of older formats like AVI or MOV. Reduce the video resolution without compromising on quality. Shorten video length and break up longer videos into smaller clips. Host videos on third-party platforms like YouTube or Vimeo to reduce website storage space.
#4 Fonts
To use fonts for a sustainable website, you can:
Limit the number of font styles and use them consistently. Choose web-safe fonts to reduce the need for additional font files. Use font subsets to reduce the size of font files. Avoid using custom fonts to ensure maximum compatibility and sustainability. Consider font size and weight to reduce energy consumption.
#5 SEO
Good SEO practices can have several environmental benefits. By optimizing website loading times, reducing the size of media files, and using sustainable web hosting, you can reduce energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions. Additionally, these practices can extend the lifespan of electronic devices, minimize electronic waste, and demonstrate your commitment to sustainability, which can help build a positive brand image.
#6 Lazy Loading
Lazy loading is a technique that delays the loading of non-critical resources on a web page, such as images, until they are needed. This can help to improve website performance and reduce the amount of data that needs to be downloaded, which in turn can have several environmental benefits, including:
Reduced data usage: By delaying the loading of non-critical resources, lazy loading can reduce the amount of data that needs to be downloaded, which can help to reduce the amount of energy used by servers and devices during the data transfer process.
Improved website speed: By prioritizing the loading of critical resources, lazy loading can help to improve website speed and reduce page load times, which can lead to a better user experience and increased engagement.
#7 AMP
AMP can benefit a sustainable website in several ways. It can reduce energy consumption and improve website speed, leading to a better user experience and increased engagement. Additionally, AMP pages are prioritized by search engines, which can increase your website's reach and promote sustainable practices.
#8 Declutter Site
Decluttering your website can improve sustainability by reducing energy consumption and improving the user experience. A cluttered website can slow down page loading times, leading to increased energy consumption, while a simplified design can promote engagement and align with your brand values. Additionally, a decluttered website can be easier to maintain and update over time, reducing costs and energy usage.
#9 Hosting
Choosing a sustainable website host is important for reducing the environmental impact of your website. Opt for a host that uses energy-efficient servers, powered by renewable energy sources, offers carbon offsetting programs, and allows you to monitor and optimize your resource usage. By doing so, you can minimize energy consumption, lower carbon emissions, and promote a more sustainable future.
#10 Dark Mode
Dark mode on a website is a good feature for sustainability because it reduces energy consumption of devices with OLED and AMOLED screens by displaying black backgrounds instead of white. This results in lower energy consumption and potentially longer battery life for devices, leading to a reduction in overall energy consumption and carbon emissions.
